Frequently Asked Questions

Practical questions about hot water usage, heater types, and the calculator's assumptions.

How much water do people on average use for showering in Singapore?

›

It depends on showerhead flow rate and how long each shower lasts. PUB requires new showerheads to flow no more than 9 L/min; "Very Good" rated fittings flow ≤ 7 L/min. Most modern HDB / condo bathrooms land in this range.

Per shower (mixed water at the showerhead)

Showerhead Flow 8-min shower 12-min shower
Low-flow / water-efficient6–7 L/min~50 L~80 L
Standard9–10 L/min~75 L~115 L
Rainshower / older fittings12–18 L/min~110 L~180 L

Per person per day (mixed shower water)

  • Light user (1 short shower): ~50 L
  • Average SG household: ~70–90 L
  • Heavy user (long or multiple showers): 120–180 L
For the calculator's Hot Water per Person (L/day) field:

Use the mixed water at the tap — the actual water you shower with. That number, multiplied by the delivery temperature minus the cold inlet, gives the energy needed regardless of whether your heater is instant-regulated or a storage tank with a tap mixer. Default 80 L/person/day covers a ~10-minute shower at 8 L/min plus a bit of basin / washing-up usage.

My heater has a regulator I set to 38°C — doesn't it only heat to that point?

›

Yes — if your heater is an instant heater with an output regulator (most modern Rinnai gas instant, Joven, and many electric instant units), it modulates the burner / heating element to deliver water at exactly the dial temperature. Set 38°C, get 38°C at the tap. No mixing happens at the tap mixer for hot water — you just open the hot side.

That's a completely different control philosophy from a storage tank:

Storage tank (gas or electric)

  • Tank held at ~60°C always (kills Legionella).
  • Dial on the unit sets the tank temperature.
  • You mix hot + cold at the shower mixer to get ~40°C.
  • ΔT to heat = 60 − 28 = 32°C; you draw less hot water (mixing dilutes it).

Instant heater with output regulator

  • No tank. Heater modulates output in real time.
  • Dial sets the delivery temperature.
  • You open the hot tap directly — no mixing needed.
  • ΔT to heat = delivery − cold (e.g. 38 − 28 = 10°C); you draw the full tap volume.

Why the energy is similar (but not identical)

Heating any mass of water by a temperature rise takes the same energy (m × c × ΔT). For an 80 L shower at 40°C from 28°C cold:

  • Instant @ 40°C: 80 L × 12°C = 960 L·°C
  • Tank @ 60°C (mix 30 L hot + 50 L cold): 30 L × 32°C = 960 L·°C — same.

But the tank also pays standby loss (~25–30 kWh/month for a 50 L tank in SG ambient) regardless of whether you shower. That's the real efficiency penalty of a tank vs. an instant heater.

How the calculator handles this:

The Hot Water Delivery Temp field is the temperature you actually use — your dial setting (instant) or your shower mixing temp (tank). Heating energy is computed from (delivery − cold inlet), which works for both. Tank standby loss is added separately, assuming the tank is held at 60°C internally. Set tank capacity = 0 for an instant heater.

Sources: PUB water-saving guidance, manufacturer flow specs, and household water-usage surveys. Numbers are typical-case estimates; your actual usage will vary.
