How the Calculator Works

Every formula and constant the ROI calculator uses, in the order they fire. Verify the math against your own bills, or compute scenarios by hand.

In one paragraph

We estimate monthly hot-water energy from household size, daily litres, and temperatures. We add tank standby loss for storage heaters. We multiply each scenario's monthly kWh by its tariff, escalate yearly, and compute the cumulative savings against the net upfront investment. The first month where cumulative savings cover the investment is the break-even month.

1Net Investment

The upfront cost to switch, minus what you'd have spent anyway, minus any cashback. 

Inet = Cconv − Crepl − Ccb

Defaults: 1199 − 500 − 0 = $699

2Hot Water Demand (Heating Energy)

Energy needed to raise the daily volume of water from cold inlet to delivery temperature. Uses the standard m × c × ΔT form, with m in litres (water density 1 kg/L). 

ΔTheat  = max(0, Tdel − Tcold)
Lday     = N × Lp
Eheat    = Lday × ΔTheat × c       [kWh / day]

Defaults: ΔT = 40 − 28 = 12°C; Lday = 4 × 80 = 320 L; Eheat = 320 × 12 × 0.001163 ≈ 4.47 kWh/day

Heating energy is identical for both heaters — same water, same temperature rise. The differences appear in the next step (standby) and in the per-heater tariff.

3Standby Loss (per Heater)

Storage tanks leak heat to ambient continuously. Instant heaters have no tank, so no standby loss. The "effective tank temperature" depends on heater type: 

Teff = Ttank   if heaterType = "Storage tank — with shower mixer"
       Tdel    otherwise
       (tank-no-mixer: delivery = setpoint;
        instant: tank capacity is 0, so the value doesn't matter)

ΔTtank     = max(0, Teff − Tamb)
Selec      = Ve × ΔTtank × s        [kWh / day]
Sgas       = Vg × ΔTtank × s        [kWh / day]

Example (50 L electric tank with mixer at 60°C, ambient 28°C): Selec = 50 × 32 × 0.0006 = 0.96 kWh/day (~29 kWh/month).

4Monthly Energy per Heater

Uelec = (Eheat + Selec) × D       [kWh / month]
Ugas  = (Eheat + Sgas)  × D       [kWh / month]

Defaults (with mixer, 60°C tank): Uelec = (4.47 + 0.96) × 30 ≈ 162.8 kWh/mo; Ugas = 4.47 × 30 ≈ 134.0 kWh/mo.

5Year-by-Year Tariffs & Annual Savings

Each tariff compounds at its own annual rate. Costs accrue only during active usage months. Savings are the electric scenario's cost minus the gas scenario's cost. 

Pe(N) = Pe × (1 + re)N−1 × (1 + g)
Pg(N) = Pg × (1 + rg)N−1 × (1 + g)

Costelec(N) = Uelec × Pe(N) × Ma
Costgas(N)  = Ugas  × Pg(N) × Ma

A(N) = Costelec(N) − Costgas(N)         [annual savings, year N]

Defaults: A(1) = 162.8 × 0.2972 × 8 − 134.0 × 0.2389 × 8 ≈ 387.1 − 256.1 = $131/yr.

6Cumulative Savings & Net Position

Cum(N) = Σk=1..N A(k)
Net(N) = Cum(N) − Inet

These produce the 10-year projection table. The row where Net(N) ≥ 0 contains the break-even month.

7Break-Even Month & Payback Period

Walk forward month-by-month. Each active month adds 1/Ma of that year's annual savings. Off-months contribute zero. The first month the running total reaches the net investment is the break-even month. 

cum ← 0
for m from 1 to 600:
    yearIdx     ← ⌈m / 12⌉
    monthInYear ← ((m − 1) mod 12) + 1
    if monthInYear ≤ Ma:
        cum ← cum + A(yearIdx) / Ma
    if cum ≥ Inet:
        return m   ← break-even month

Payback (years) = m / 12

The 600-month cap (50 years) prevents an infinite loop in pathological cases. Special results:

  • Inet ≤ 0 → "Already paid back" (replacement cost ≥ conversion cost)
  • A(1) ≤ 0 → "No payback" (gas not cheaper, or standby ≥ savings)
  • Loop hits the cap → "> 50 years"

Assumptions & Limitations

  • Equal heater efficiency. Heating energy is treated as identical for gas and electric. In reality, gas heaters lose ~15–20% up the flue, electric is ~95–98% efficient. Not modelling that overstates gas's per-kWh advantage. See the How Heaters Work page for the efficiency-adjusted view.
  • Standby loss factor s = 0.0006 is a typical residential value; well-insulated tanks are lower, ageing tanks higher.
  • Active months are assumed contiguous (months 1..Ma of each year). Off-months contribute zero usage.
  • Tariffs compound annually at a constant rate. Real tariffs are revised quarterly and can swing in either direction.
  • No standing charges or fixed fees are modelled. If switching off gas eliminates a $15–20/month standing charge, real savings exceed the calculator's output.
  • Hot water demand is assumed constant year-round (subject to active-months gating). Seasonal variation is not modelled.
  • 30 days/month is a calendar simplification. Off by ~1.7% from a true mean (30.44).
