What the trends tell you

  • Both tariffs are pegged to fuel-import prices — gas tracks LNG, electricity tracks the natural gas SP buys to generate. They tend to move together.
  • 2015–2016 dip: oil & LNG collapsed; electricity hit ~19¢/kWh (lowest in the series) and gas hit ~16¢/kWh.
  • 2022–2024 surge: Russia / Ukraine + LNG supply squeeze pushed electricity to a peak of 32.58¢/kWh in 2024 Q1.
  • The gap (electric − gas) is fairly stable: typically 7–9¢/kWh in recent years. That gap is what drives the ROI calculator's running-cost savings.
